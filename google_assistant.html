Google Assistant User Research

A UX research project partnered with Google Assistant product team

Project Type

Team project of User Research course partnered with Google Assistant

Team

Ashley Fleckenstein, Courtney Snede, Yankun Wang, Kay Zhu

Duration

3 months

Role

UX Researcher

Project Overview

Google Assistant (GA) is a voice-controlled virtual assistant for mobile phones and other smart devices. In January 2019 Google announced that Assistant will soon be installed on over 1 billion devices. It supports 30 languages in 80 countries.

Since Google Assistant is such a widely used, complex product, our stakeholders at Google helped us to narrow the scope of this project. Our primary research goals for these usability tests are to:

(1) Understand better how college students, 18-26, interact with mobile voice assistants.

(2) Identify any unmet needs related to productivity tasks.

We will also uncover how voice assistants integrate into the lives of these users, and discover what changes to the product may persuade non-users to adopt it.
