Atlas Schedule Builder

Web application design that supports efficient and informative course schedule building

Project Type

UX Design internship - Web app design

Role

I solo designed this project with the help of my mentor David Corneail

Duration

6 weeks

Skills

Conceptual Model, Design Jam, Competitive Analysis, Wireframing, Prototyping

Intro

Atlas

Atlas is a data visualization tool that shows past enrollment data about U-M courses, instructors, and majors to guide U-M students, instructors, and staff in decision-making. Atlas is used by more than 85% U of M students.

Problem

How to make academic schedule building a pleasant process

In the coming future, Altas will replace the U of M current course register system Wolverine Access. As the first step of the replacing process, a future milestone for Atlas is to develop a schedule builder that would incorporate saved courses from Atlas and would allow students to build a personalized course schedule. I was asked to lead the research and design of this project.

Currently, students had a lot of complaints about the Wolverine Access system and its schedule builder feature. The challenge of this project includes outlining user pain points, demonstrating how students would plan a schedule in Atlas, and turning schedule building into a more pleasant process.

Design Overview

Efficient and informative schedule builder

Atlas schedule builder allows student users to browse through courses, review rich course data and build personalized course schedules with selected courses. Users can add courses to the schedule through searching or from their course collections in Atlas. The schedule builder also provides various academic data to support decision making throughout the process.

Solution
Responsive schedule building

When building their schedules, users have the Select Course panel on the left which serves like a “shopping cart”. Users can toggle to turn courses on and off and switch different sections.

The schedule will update based on the Select Course panel so that users can try multiple combinations until that find the ideal schedule.

Conceptual Model

Understanding user's mental model

Schedule Builder is a familiar concept to our target audiences. However, academic schedule building is a complex process and people have different ways of doing it.

In order to understand how the target audiences think about a schedule builder, I facilitated a workshop with college students to build up the conceptual model. I generated a conceptual model based on the models provided by the users. The conceptual model helps me understand what information is necessary and what features the users expect.

Hallway Interview

Collecting qualitative data

To further understand the pain points of building academic schedules, I conducted some hallway interviews with some target audiences. I asked them about their behaviors when building a schedule, what they don’t like during the process, and what features they expect in the coming schedule builder.

Competitive Analysis

Learning from existing products

Design Jam

Participatory design with target audiences

Our team designed and facilitated a design jam of the schedule builder. We invited students from different schools to participate in the design process. We also invited other team members such as the developers, data scientists, faculties, and the learning experience designers as the judges.

We wanted to involve the stakeholders in the design process to help ensure the result meets their needs and is usable. It was also a great activity to identify potential design opportunities.
